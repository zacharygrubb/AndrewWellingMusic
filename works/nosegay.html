NOSEGAY i, ii

These two movements were written for the James Madison University large ensemble competition.

Nosegay is a collection of pieces designed to challenge the wind ensemble in unique, and unexpected ways. The world of band music can be rather insular, and for whatever reason does not have as much stylistic diversity as other genres. I felt it was important that I contribute something different which brings a new voice to the band world and to widen the horizons of this flexible and rich ensemble.

These short movements are sonic glimpses into a variety of musical paragons (aesthetic references to well known contemporary styles and techniques), and despite the extreme differences between each, they are all related by a precise but albeit veiled architectural scheme. There are plenty of hints littered throughout the score which point to the pieceâ€™s construction method. It is a musical puzzle which begs to be solved.

i

The first movement is an evocation of the space, which rouses the senses with striking monolithic chords. It unfolds and flourishes, as though catching flame. The listener is swept away in an all consuming and rapturous ascent. The piece departs as mysteriously as it began, leaving us with questions and opening our ears to new sonic possibilities.

ii

The second movement is an explosion of frenetic energy - a virile flow, leaping and bounding over itself only to cascade into brilliant shimmering sonorities. This brief movement is extremely compact, brutal, and exciting.

iii and onward...

The third movement will be an exercise in delicacy and melodic sensitivity . It will challenge performers to master quarter tones in a melodic context. The goal is to further the skills of musicians and to make their musical lexicon more inclusive.
